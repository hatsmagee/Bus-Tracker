🚌 About this dashboard
A live map of every Hele-On Bus in service right now, built on the County's official GTFS-realtime feed. It reads the whole fleet from one VehiclePositions feed, shows the agency's own predicted arrival times (TripUpdates), and layers a small transformer on top that learns each stop's typical timing from history.
🎨 How to read it
Bus icons glide smoothly along the road between GPS updates (snapped to the route, moving forward at the bus's real pace) with the route number always upright. Buses that overlap fan out so none is hidden, and ones whose GPS has gone quiet fade to a ghost at their last-known spot. Blue stops are official GTFS stops — click one for approaching buses and history. Amber rings are observed stops: spots where buses repeatedly stop that aren't official, learned from GPS history. Direction chevrons stream ahead of each bus along the road, showing where it's about to go. A weather symbol above each bus shows live conditions there, and the colored bar shows how full it is. A soft amber/red glow marks congestion — where a bus is crawling well below the normal speed for that spot (inferred from our own GPS history, since no public traffic-signal feed exists anywhere). The Fleet tab lists every bus the agency knows — live, idle, or dormant — with full telemetry.

🧠 The predictor
Headline arrival times come straight from the agency's official realtime predictions. On top of those, a 12-token single-head transformer (trained server-side every time a bus reaches a stop) learns a correction from history. Inputs are cyclical time-of-day and day-of-week (sin/cos), distance to the next stop, and speed; outputs are ETA adjustments for the next 5 stops.
